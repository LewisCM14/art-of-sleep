Back

When sleeping on your back, your neck and spine remain in a neutral position, meaning you're less likely to experience sleep related neck pain. If you suffer from heartburn, sleeping on your back with your head slightly elevated will also help elevate this.


However, sleeping on your back can cause, or aggravate, existing lower back pain. It can also worsen the effects of snoring and sleep apnoea.


Women should also avoid sleeping in this position whilst pregnant.

When sleeping on a firmer surface, the bones absorb most of the pressure, meaning there is less stress on muscles, it also helps prevent your lower back from collapsing, which can increase oxygen intake and help with snoring and sleep apnoea.


Please be aware, if you suffer from back conditions such as arthritis or scoliosis, a mattress that is too firm could be negatively impacting these problems.


A heavier person may also find a firm mattress deceptively soft.
