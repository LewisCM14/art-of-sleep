Although sleeping on your stomach may help with snoring, it generally aggravates other medical conditions.


Your neck and spine are not in a neutral position when you sleep on your stomach, which may lead to neck and back pain.


Stomach sleeping can also put pressure on nerves and cause numbness or tingling.

It is generally recommended that you switch positions if you sleep on your stomach, however, if you can't break the habit, a thin pillow or no pillow at all will help with the angle of your neck.


A pillow under your pelvis will also help keep your spine in a neutral position.


Stomach sleepers tend to fare better on firmer mattress, that still offer a degree of support. Helping keep the neck and spine in as neutral position as possible.
